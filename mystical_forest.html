Deep within the Elderwald, where sunlight filters through the canopy like honey through gauze, the trees speak in languages older than man. Their roots weave together beneath the earth, forming a network older than any kingdom. To step into this wood is to step into a memory. Whose memory? The trees do not say. They wait.

At dusk, the fireflies rise from the ferns like embers from a forgotten fire. They do not merely glow — they speak in light, tracing paths in the air that only the forest understands. The old folk say that if you follow a firefly long enough, you will find yourself at the heart of the wood. But no one who reaches the heart has ever returned to tell of it.
